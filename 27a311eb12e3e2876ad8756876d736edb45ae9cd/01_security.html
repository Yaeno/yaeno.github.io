INTRODUCTION

The Foundation of Data Management

I'm the lead designer for Platform and Platform Security. My focus is on providing a unified platform experience of configuration and monitoring, as well as comprehensive security to protect against infrastructure-level attacks.

CHALLENGES AND SOLUTIONS

Challenge 1

Share Common Vision

The biggest challenge and risk is developing products that users don't want. This can be a costly endeavor, as it requires resources and time. To mitigate this risk, it is important to understand users, open to feedback and adjust the design accordingly.

Business Solution

As a designer, I don't just read the Product Manager's (PM) priority list. Instead, I strive to gain a deeper understanding of the business behind it. To do this, I actively participate in customer calls and develop an Opportunity Canvas with the PM and engineers.

Design Solution

To ensure the discovery team—including the PM, engineer lead, and designer—share the same vision, I invited everyone to a 6-8-5 sketching session to brainstorm high-level ideas together during the ideation stage. When moving on to the design stage, everyone on the team contributes to the story map and provides critiques.

Challenge 2

Do The Right Things

To ensure that our delivered products benefit our customers, I must not only understand our users and align our product vision with different stakeholders, but also leverage various resources. In addition to following our design process, I have identified two primary areas that require my attention.

Area 1

Develop Minimum Lovable Product

We need to plan carefully to ensure we can meet the feasibility and release time frame. To that end, our organization has decided to plan and build the Minimum Lovable Product (MLP): a complete release that is as small as possible.

Area 2

Keep an Eye on API

As a designer, I requested to join the API review, since GUI and API are closely intertwined. Poor API design can disrupt user workflow, which in turn impacts GUI design.

Challenge 3

Transfer Technical Mindsets to User-Friendly Language

Platform-related topics can often be complex and use technical language. Our challenge is to simplify the workflow and make it more understandable for users.

Solution

One approach is to encapsulate complexity and create logical concepts that can act as proxies for communication. This enables users to avoid having to comprehend complex ideas, while also reducing the risk of errors.

Challenge 4

Consider Scalability

As our business expands, we must consider scalability from both the back-end and front-end perspectives. This requires careful thought and planning.

Solution

I lead and manage the design of common components, with an emphasis on scalability. For example, how to deal with a menu that can potentially have thousands of items.

Challenge 5

Coordinate Multiple Teams And Deliver On Time

I need coordinate with the marketing, PM, and engineering teams to understand and plan the scope of urgent projects.

Solution

As a manager, the first step in coordinating is to understand the requirements, limitations, and delivery expectations of each team. This understanding helps in planning and prioritizing tasks, setting deadlines, and ensuring timely delivery of work with good quality.
