INTRODUCTION

From Zero to One

When I joined the team, designers were focused on delivery, but there was no mechanism to test if our work was valid and efficient. Furthermore, there were no channels for designers to connect with users; the only vehicle at the time was communicating with customers via PMs.

Achievements

• Established usability testing mechanism in the company;
• Managing usability testings since 2020;
• Initiated and advocated user research in the company.

BRIEFING

Usability Testing

Problem

The team needs an index to monitor the ease use of features.

Challenge

• Develop a solution from scratch;
• Deliver the results in 2 weeks.

Output

• Established usability testing mechanism;
• Index score increased from 6.8 to 8.2;
• Tested 32 projects and continuously evolved the process.

PROCESS

Divide-and-Conquer

I broke the problem into smaller pieces:

Develop a testing process

• What is the most approachable and efficient testing method?
• What is the testing process?

Develop a rating system

• Use an existing system or develop a new one?
• How to apply the rating system to our design process?

Deliver results

• How to calculate the index?
• How to present the results?
• How to manage follow-up actions?

Manage projects

• What resources are needed to finish testing?
• How to distribute resources?
• How to deliver the results on time?

DELIVERY

Solution

We used a traditional task-based methodology to test projects, with at least 3 participants, ideally customers or field, rate the recorded videos by the heuristic measurement system PURE, and produce a final index score.

Tests were conducted twice for each project - once after the prototype was completed, and again after code was finished. This enabled us to interweave Agile Development and Validated Learning Cycle to keep improving.

Advocation

To highlight the significance of testing and ease design team members' stress when their designs are assessed, I promote a culture of measurement within the team. Here are two ideas I share with team members:

• "Nail it before you expand it."

• "Design as if you are correct, but test as if you are wrong."

Artifacts

In addition to process development, I created script templates, rating sheets, summary templates, and an index heat map.

CHALLENGES

1. Find Appropriate Data Visualizations

The application provides the flexibility for users to tweak the meal ingredients based on their set goals. I was looking for appropriate formats to visualize the relationships between the goals and the current energy and nutrition distribution.

The design goal is to present the information and relationship in ways that are accurate, clean and understandable. The image below displays the iteration process.

2. What Is The Appropriate Navigation ?

The navigation of the Ketogenic Meal Planner is straightforward, which surfaces the four main functions to the users.

I compared the pros and cons of each style of navigation, and tested these with users. The final decision was to go to a full-screen navigation pattern, which is best for achieving simplicity. Since this application is task-oriented, the full-screen navigation can directly reveal information without overwhelming the users.
