BRIEF

Ketogenic Meal Plan

This iOS application is designed to facilitate parents whose kids are lacking glucose transporters to plan ketogenic meals which generate glucose via burning fats.

This project is under development. I interviewed users, built high-resolution mockups using Sketch, and coded high-fidelity prototype using Framer JS.

THE ORIGIN OF THE IDEA

Anxiety Free Meal Planning

Back in April, I visited my school friend Chloe, whose daughter Angela is turning 5 this year. Angela cannot speak because her body lacks glucose transporters which prevent the glucose from being transported to her brain. Therefore, Angela's brain development is slower.

Doctors suggested Angela try a Ketogenic diet that can transfer glucose via burning fat. As a mother, Chloe has been planning every meal via an Excel spreadsheet since last year.

Chloe's Meal Plan

However, Excel is not portable and cannot automatically calculate the amounts of nutrition that need to be taken for each meal.

We decided to build an iOS application that can effortlessly plan Ketogenic Meals to save time and the effort needed to maintain an Excel spreadsheet.

UNDERSTAND USERS

Demographic

For the first release, our target users are the parents who plan the ketogenic meals for their child to treat the problem of poor glucose transportation.

Interviews

I interviewed three moms to understand their habits of daily meal planning. I summarized the interviews into five main points.

When planning meals, everyone strictly follows the nutrition proportion (Fat : Sugar + Protein) their doctors suggest, especially at the beginning of the meal planning.

Most of the interviewees use Excel to track nutrition. However, when planning each meal, it is difficult to calculate the amounts of food that can be taken based on the set goals.

Interviewees will save the most nutritious and best tasting recipes.

Daily data can be useful to track the healing process, and monthly data can help troubleshoot problems when new symptoms occur.

Interviewees need the ability to constantly tweak the ingredients and leverage their proportion and the taste of the meal.

User Journey

In order to genuinely understand users' needs, I built a journey map to tell the stories from the users' perspective. The map highlights both the physical and emotional process when planning ketogenic meals.

Story Map

Based on the results of the interviews, and in order to break down the feedback to smaller actions, I built a user story map which slices the main topics into smaller, actionable tasks.

DESIGN

Introducing Assistance

In the mobile age, the Ketogenic Meal Planner assists by making meal planning fast, simple and efficient. The planner can automatically generate recipes based on specified ingredients and effortlessly tracks your nutritional intake.

Simple Navigation

The navigation of the Ketogenic Meal Planner is straightforward, which surfaces the four main functions to the users.

New Recipe Creation

The application can automatically create recipes based on the entered ingredients.

1. Set Meal Goals

2. Add Ingredients

3. Tweak Recipe Based on the Goals

Saved Recipe

Users can find saved recipes and read their ingredients.

Data

Tracking data is important to observe and measure the healing effects.

1. Tracking Daily Data

2. Track Monthly Data for Each Nutrition

Food

The application provides flexibility for users to search the FDA food list or enter a custom food item.

CHALLENGES

1. Find Appropriate Data Visualizations

The application provides the flexibility for users to tweak the meal ingredients based on their set goals. I was looking for appropriate formats to visualize the relationships between the goals and the current energy and nutrition distribution.

The design goal is to present the information and relationship in ways that are accurate, clean and understandable. The image below displays the iteration process.

2. What Is The Appropriate Navigation ?

The navigation of the Ketogenic Meal Planner is straightforward, which surfaces the four main functions to the users.

I compared the pros and cons of each style of navigation, and tested these with users. The final decision was to go to a full-screen navigation pattern, which is best for achieving simplicity. Since this application is task-oriented, the full-screen navigation can directly reveal information without overwhelming the users.
